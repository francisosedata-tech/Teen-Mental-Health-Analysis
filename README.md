## 📋 Project Overview

*Voluntary Data Analysis Project*  

This project explores the relationship between *social media usage, **mental wellbeing, and **academic performance* among *1,200 teens*. The goal is to uncover actionable insights that can support parents, educators, and youth development organizations in promoting healthier digital habits. Using SQL for data extraction and Power BI for visualization, the study reveals moderate but concerning risks associated with high social media engagement. 

*Status*: Completed (Voluntary Project)  
*Date*: July 2026

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze depression rates, stress, anxiety, and addiction levels among teens.
- Examine the impact of social media usage on sleep, screen time, and academic performance.
- Identify high-risk groups and provide data-driven recommendations.
- Demonstrate end-to-end data analysis and visualization skills using Power BI.

## 📊 Key Insights

### Mental Wellbeing
- Overall *Depression Rate: **2.58%*
- Average *Social Media Usage: **4.54 hours/day*
- *42.58%* of teens fall into the high-usage category
- Females recorded a slightly higher depression rate (2.91%) than males (2.28%)
- High social media usage is associated with elevated *stress (5.6), **anxiety (5.7), and **addiction (5.7)* levels
- *Gender Insight:* Females represent *43.93%* of depression cases despite balanced representation.
  
### Academic Performance
- Average *Academic Performance Score: **2.99*
- Average *Sleep Hours: **6.45*
- Average *Screen Time: **1.74 hours*
- High social media users show lower academic performance and reduced sleep hours
- *Instagram* is the most used platform (411 users), followed by *TikTok* (398)

### High-Risk Patterns
- Younger teens with high social media usage are particularly vulnerable to negative outcomes.
- Clear negative correlation between excessive social media use, sleep quality, and academic results.

## Stakeholder-Specific Recommendations & Actions

### For *Schools & Educators*
- *Implement Digital Wellness Curriculum:* Integrate weekly modules on mindful social media use and sleep hygiene for students with high usage (>4.5 hours/day).
- *Early Screening:* Use similar dashboards to flag at-risk students (high media usage + low sleep) for counseling.
- *Parental Engagement:* Host workshops showing these visualizations to encourage home-school collaboration.

### For *Parents & Guardians*
- *Set Usage Boundaries:* Encourage <4 hours daily social media with family screen-free times, especially before bed.
- *Monitor & Communicate:* Focus conversations on quality over quantity of usage; promote physical activity as a buffer.
- *Model Behavior:* Parents should demonstrate balanced digital habits.

### For *Mental Health Organizations & Policymakers*
- *Targeted Campaigns:* Launch awareness drives focused on female teens and popular platforms (Instagram/TikTok).
- *Intervention Programs:* Develop or scale apps/tools that track usage and suggest breaks, aiming to reduce high-usage group risks.
- *Policy Advocacy:* Push for platform accountability on teen mental health features and support school-based data monitoring tools.

### For *Ed-Tech Companies & Researchers*
- *Product Features:* Build dashboards like this into learning platforms for real-time wellbeing insights.
- *Further Analysis:* Conduct longitudinal studies to measure intervention impact and explore protective factors (e.g., sports, strong social interaction).
